# Vyaz — User Personas

*Last updated: June 28, 2026*

---

## Persona 1: The Book Consumer

**Who they are:**
A professional, student, or lifelong learner who wants to consume book knowledge but can't find the time to read. They're self-aware about this — they don't lack interest, they lack hours. Their reading list keeps growing, their available time keeps shrinking.

**What they want:**
- Consume a specific title in a finite, predictable time (30-60 min)
- Find a credible narrator who has genuinely internalized the book
- Ask their own questions — not get a generic summary
- Walk away feeling like they "got" the book enough to apply it, discuss it, or decide whether to read it fully

**Their pain:**
- "I have 50 books on my list and I'll never finish them"
- "Summaries feel shallow — I want depth without the time commitment"
- "I finished the book but I'm not sure I understood it — I have no one to discuss it with"

**How they use Vyaz:**
1. Browse books → check ratings + reviews → find a narrator
2. Book a 30-min session (1:1 or join a group)
3. Show up, ask questions, get the substance
4. Review the narrator afterward

**What makes them stay:**
- Narrators who are genuinely knowledgeable (not reading from SparkNotes)
- The ability to ask specific, personal questions
- Consistency — same quality every session

---

## Persona 2: The Book Narrator

**Who they are:**
A bibliophile who reads voraciously and has deeply internalized one or more titles. They don't just read books — they think about them, connect ideas across them, and love discussing them. They're confident in their ability to explain, contextualize, and narrate a book compellingly.

**What they want:**
- A platform to share their knowledge and passion for books
- Validation of their ability to narrate (verification builds credibility)
- Start doing this for free, get paid for it as demand grows
- Build a reputation — become the go-to person for specific titles
- Potentially do this full-time if the opportunity is there

**Their pain:**
- "I read 50+ books a year and have no one to discuss them with"
- "I could teach this book better than most summaries, but there's no platform for that"
- "I'd love to get paid for what I already do for free at book clubs"

**How they use Vyaz:**
1. Sign up as narrator → go through verification for each title
2. Set availability → connect Google Calendar
3. Receive session bookings → host 1:1 or group sessions
4. Build ratings and reviews → attract more listeners
5. Get paid per session (future)

**What makes them stay:**
- Demand — real people booking real sessions
- Recognition — ratings, reviews, "top narrator" status
- Income potential — the possibility of turning passion into livelihood
- Community — connecting with other narrators and readers

---

## The Flywheel

```
More narrators → more titles covered → more listeners find what they want
     ↑                                              ↓
More income/recognition ← more sessions booked ← more listeners sign up
```

The marketplace works when:
- **Listeners** find narrators for the titles they want (catalog coverage)
- **Narrators** get enough bookings to stay engaged (demand density)
- **Quality** stays high (verification + reviews filter out bad narrators)

---

## Key Insight

The narrator is not a summarizer. They are a **conversational guide** — someone who has lived with the book long enough to answer any question about it, connect it to the listener's context, and deliver the substance in a way that a written summary never could. The difference between Vyaz and Blinkist is the difference between a conversation and a pamphlet.
