01

The thesis

Vyaz exists to carry an author's original thinking to a reader — faithfully, and in the way humans have always passed knowledge on: in conversation. Everything in this document defends that idea.

Named for Vyasa — the sage who spoke the Mahabharata and the Puranas into being — Vyaz treats the person who put pen to paper as the source of truth, and the voice as a faithful medium, never a substitute. In an era of AI slop that averages everything into sludge, Vyaz is a stand for the opposite: the genuine, the sourced, the whole.

01

Fidelity over slop

We deliver the author's actual words — verbatim. The AI narrates; it never paraphrases, summarizes, or invents. Faithfulness is the product, not a setting.

02

Conversation is oldest

Knowledge moves the way we evolved to move it — spoken, in dialogue you can interrupt and question. The epics were spoken before they were written.

03

Honor the author

The human who created the idea is named, credited, and rewarded. Vyaz elevates originators, it doesn't launder them into anonymity.

04

Rooted, then modern

Grounded in Indian intellectual heritage, delivered warm and contemporary — never academic, never cold, never gimmicky.

The one line

If a design choice makes Vyaz feel like generated content rather than a real person's genuine work, faithfully carried — it's wrong, however pretty.

02

Personality & voice

Vyaz is a warm, encouraging tutor with real substance — a patient mentor who happens to be deeply well-read. Warm, never saccharine. Confident, never arrogant. Plain-spoken, never dumbed-down.

We are

Warm · patient · precise · respectful of both the author and the learner · quietly confident · unhurried.

We are not

Hype-y · salesy · jargon-heavy · gamified · ironic · anything that smells of the AI slop we exist to oppose.

Voice & tone in practice

Write like this

"Let's pick up where we left off."

"Narrated from Ben Horowitz's own words."

"That chapter didn't load. Tap to try again."

Plain, human, specific. Credit the author by name. Errors say what happened and the way out.

Not like this

"🚀 Supercharge your learning journey!"

"Our AI summarized this book for you."

"Oops! Something went wrong 😬"

Hype, emoji confetti, and — worst of all — implying we summarized or replaced the author.

The tell: our language always keeps the author present. We never say Vyaz "knows" or "wrote" — Vyaz carries, narrates, reads. The credit points outward, to the human.

03

The name & the seal

Vyaz → Vyasa: the archetypal narrator-compiler of Indian knowledge. The name carries the whole thesis in four letters — the trusted voice that faithfully delivers the great works.

The mark: a seal, not a logo-swoosh

Our symbol is a seal — the hanko/chhāp, the stamp of authenticity that both East-Asian and Indian manuscript traditions press onto genuine work. In Vyaz the seal means one thing: this is the real, sourced original.

व

A red seal bearing the Devanagari va (व). It is the only place the brand shouts — small, deliberate, earned. Everything else is black ink on white paper.

This is why red survives as the one accent — but disciplined. It is not a "highlight color" sprinkled around; it is the seal of the authentic, and it is spent sparingly (see §5).

04

Positioning & who we're not

Vyaz is a premium AI study companion for serious learners in India — students, aspirants, and professionals who want depth, not shortcuts. Priced to feel premium yet stay reachable.

We are

A faithful narrator of real books, in conversation.

A place that credits and rewards authors.

A calm, premium study companion.

We are not

A summary/"book-in-15-min" app.

A generic AI chatbot that answers about books from its own training.

A gamified, streak-pushing consumer app.

The seal rule

The seal marks the authentic and the active: the brand mark, the live/recording indicator, the selected item, inline "book verbatim" highlights, and at most one primary action per view. Never a large background fill, never two competing reds on one screen, never body text. When in doubt, the workhorse is ink — spend the seal like it costs money.

Light & dark

Dark mode is a clean inversion, not a warm tint: background #0A0A0A, text #FFFFFF, and the seal brightens to #FF453A so it still reads at full contrast on black. Same meaning, inverted ground — black ink becomes white ink, white paper becomes black paper, the seal stays the seal. This page is showing you your current scheme.

Semantic states vs. the seal

Error red (oxblood) is deliberately darker and more brownish than the brand seal, so "something's wrong" never gets confused with "this is authentic." Success/warning lean on sage/ochre — plain, functional, never neon, never competing with the seal for attention.

Brand typeface

Adopt the IBM Plex family — Plex Serif (headings), Plex Sans (body), Plex Mono (data). It's open-source, warm-modern, and crucially ships Devanagari — so Latin and Indic set in harmony, honoring the roots without a second font. (Fallbacks: Georgia / system-sans / system-mono. This page previews with fallbacks; the app should self-host Plex.)

Type scale & rules

  • Scale (1.25): 12 · 13 · 14 · 16 (body) · 20 · 25 · 31 · 39 · 49. Stay on it.
  • Headings: serif, weight 600, tight leading (~1.1), text-wrap: balance.
  • Body: sans, weight 400, leading ~1.6, line length ≤ 68 characters.
  • Labels/eyebrows: mono, uppercase, letter-spacing ~0.1em, in stone or seal-deep.
  • Numerals in tables/timers use tabular-nums.

08

Shape, line & elevation

Zen restraint: we separate things with hairlines and space before we reach for shadows. Surfaces are paper, not floating glass.

  • Radius: sm 6px (inputs/chips) · md 10px (buttons/cards) · lg 14px (panels) · pill (tags). Modest — no pill-everything, no sharp-brutalist either.
  • Borders: 1px line hairlines are the default separator.
  • Elevation: one soft, neutral shadow token for genuinely floating things (modals, menus). Cards prefer a border over a shadow.
  • Seal never outlines everything — no red rails on cards (that's the cliché we avoid); the seal appears as a mark within, not a frame around.

09

Icon, imagery & the mark

  • Icons: line icons, ~1.5px stroke, drawn in ink (Lucide is already in use — keep it). Icons are quiet; they don't get the seal color unless they express an active/authentic state.
  • Imagery: favor ink-and-paper textures, manuscript/brushwork, real book covers. Avoid stocky 3D gradients and generic "AI" glow — they contradict the anti-slop thesis.
  • The seal motif can appear as a subtle watermark or a section mark, always small and red. It is the brand's signature gesture.
  • Author presence: wherever a book appears, the author's name is visible and dignified — never buried.
10

Motion

Motion is calm and purposeful — it explains state, it doesn't perform. If an animation is decorative, cut it (decoration reads as slop).

  • Durations: 150–250ms for UI transitions; ease-out for entrances.
  • Earn every motion: the live waveform (voice is happening), the reconnect countdown (we're recovering), a gentle fade as transcript streams in. That's about the whole budget.
  • Always honor prefers-reduced-motion — this document does.

Tags, states & inputs

12

Accessibility baseline

  • Contrast: body text meets WCAG AA (4.5:1). Ink-on-paper clears it easily; the seal is not used for small text — use seal-deep when the mark must be text/links.
  • Focus: every interactive element has a visible seal focus ring; never remove outlines without a replacement.
  • Motion: respect prefers-reduced-motion; nothing critical is conveyed by motion or color alone.
  • Targets: touch targets ≥ 44×44px. Voice controls especially — thumbs, on phones, mid-listen.
  • Language: Devanagari and Latin must render with equal care (a reason for Plex).
13

How this document governs

This is the baseline. New screens, features, and marketing derive from it — they don't reinvent it.

Precedence when choices conflict
  1. The thesis (§1) — does it honor the author and the anti-slop stand?
  2. These tokens (§5–§8) — color, type, space, shape.
  3. Component convention — reuse existing patterns before inventing.
Source of truth & changes
  • Tokens live in the app's Tailwind theme (v4 @theme); the names in §5 are the contract. Design and code share one set of variables.
  • A change to a token or a brand rule is a decision — log it in docs/DECISIONS.md (as an ADR) so the "why" survives, exactly like engineering choices.
  • When a real need contradicts this doc, that's a signal to update the doc, not to quietly diverge.
Remember

Every pixel is arguing for the same thing the product is: the genuine article, faithfully carried, delivered warm. Ink on paper. The seal, earned.
